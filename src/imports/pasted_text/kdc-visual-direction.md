# KDC Website Visual Direction Brief

## Brand Overview

KDC should feel like an editorial consultancy operating at the intersection of:
- luxury ecommerce
- CRM/lifecycle strategy
- fashion-adjacent branding
- and operational systems thinking.

The visual identity should not feel like:
- a traditional marketing agency,
- a startup SaaS company,
- or an influencer creative studio.

Instead, the site should feel:
- editorial,
- architectural,
- restrained,
- intelligent,
- and quietly luxurious.

The emotional references are:
- Toteme
- Kallmeyer
- The Row
- Another Tomorrow
- luxury gallery spaces
- fashion lookbooks
- editorial publishing
- Scandinavian/French minimalism

The site should feel:
“quiet confidence and strategic sophistication.”

Not trendy.
Not loud.
Not overly designed.

---

# Overall Art Direction

The website should prioritize:
- whitespace
- typography hierarchy
- restraint
- tonal layering
- and immersive imagery.

The design language should feel:
- calm
- cinematic
- editorial
- spacious
- and premium.

Avoid:
- bright colors
- gradients
- harsh shadows
- glossy UI
- heavy animations
- startup-style cards
- or overly corporate layouts.

Everything should feel:
- soft
- structured
- intentional
- and timeless.

---

# Color Palette

## Primary Background
Soft Editorial Off White

HEX: #FCFBF8

Use for:
- main page background
- hero background
- whitespace
- majority of layout canvas

This should feel:
- brighter than ivory
- softer than pure white
- editorial and luxury

---

## Editorial Warm Ivory
HEX: #F3F0EA

Use for:
- layered sections
- subtle content blocks
- cards
- case study backgrounds
- alternating sections

This should create tonal layering without obvious contrast.

---

## Primary Typography / Soft Black
HEX: #171717

Use for:
- logo
- hero headlines
- navigation
- large statements
- primary typography

Should feel:
- sharp but softened
- premium
- clean
- restrained

Avoid pure black.

---

## Warm Gray
HEX: #5E5954

Use for:
- secondary copy
- metadata
- subheadings
- captions
- labels
- muted navigation states

This is a critical bridge tone in the palette.

---

## Muted Espresso
HEX: #3A342F

Use sparingly for:
- hover states
- section accents
- subtle overlays
- rich contrast moments
- editorial depth

This color should feel:
- sophisticated
- architectural
- fashion-oriented
- quietly luxurious

Never overused.

---

## Light Beige Gray
HEX: #D6D0CF

Use for:
- dividers
- subtle section fills
- layered backgrounds
- soft UI framing

Should feel:
- limestone
- matte paper
- gallery stone

Avoid anything peachy or creamy.

---

# Typography System

## Logo Font
Primary:
DejaVu Serif Condensed

Secondary:
DejaVu Sans Book

Logo should feel:
- editorial
- intelligent
- understated
- fashion-adjacent

Not decorative.

---

## Hero / Editorial Headlines
Preferred:
Cormorant Garamond

Alternative:
DejaVu Serif Condensed

Use for:
- hero statements
- section intros
- manifesto text
- large editorial moments

Typography should feel:
- elegant
- literary
- calm
- restrained

Avoid:
- exaggerated luxury typography
- ultra-thin fashion fonts
- trendy serif styling

---

## Body / Secondary Typography
DejaVu Sans Book

Use for:
- body copy
- navigation
- labels
- UI structure
- pricing
- captions

Should feel:
- highly readable
- clean
- quiet
- modern

The sans serif should support the editorial serif rather than compete with it.

---

# Layout Direction

## Overall Structure
Layouts should feel:
- spacious
- asymmetrical but balanced
- editorial
- gallery-inspired

Use:
- large margins
- generous whitespace
- oversized imagery
- restrained typography blocks

Avoid:
- clutter
- dense cards
- too many modules
- boxed layouts
- obvious “agency” formatting

---

# Hero Section Direction

The homepage hero should feel cinematic and immersive.

Structure:
- full-width split imagery
- editorial fashion photography
- soft movement
- muted monochrome palette

Typography should overlay imagery in a restrained way.

The text should feel:
- calm
- confident
- intelligent
- understated

Avoid:
- oversized loud slogans
- startup copy
- flashy transitions

---

# Photography Direction

Imagery should feel:
- editorial
- fashion-oriented
- monochromatic
- emotionally restrained
- cinematic
- textured

Preferred imagery:
- grayscale fashion photography
- muted warm neutrals
- architectural interiors
- editorial portraits
- oversized garments
- soft motion blur
- tactile materials

Avoid:
- bright lifestyle imagery
- saturated colors
- overly commercial ecommerce photos
- generic stock photography

---

# UI Direction

UI should feel almost invisible.

Minimal:
- borders
- dividers
- hover states
- transitions

Avoid:
- rounded startup UI
- colorful buttons
- obvious cards
- shadows
- gradients

Buttons should feel:
- understated
- editorial
- typography-led

---

# Animation Direction

Subtle only.

Preferred:
- soft fade transitions
- slow hover states
- slight image movement
- elegant scroll pacing

Avoid:
- aggressive motion
- parallax overload
- flashy effects
- trendy animations

---

# Emotional Goal

The final website should feel like:
- a luxury editorial consultancy
- for modern ecommerce brands.

The brand should communicate:
- strategic intelligence
- aesthetic restraint
- operational sophistication
- and quiet authority.

The experience should feel:
“expensive because nothing is trying too hard.”
